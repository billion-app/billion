# Billion App - User Experience Description

This document describes how users interact with the Billion app to view and explore different types of news content. Billion is a mobile app for tracking legislative bills, court cases, executive orders, and general news.

## App Overview

Billion provides two primary ways to discover and consume content:

1. **Browse/Discovery Interface** - Search and filter content by type
2. **Vertical Video Feed** - TikTok-style scrolling experience

## Navigation Structure

The app has three main tabs at the bottom of the screen:
- **Browse** (search icon) - For discovering and searching content
- **Feed** (play icon) - For consuming content in a vertical feed
- **Settings** (cog icon) - For app preferences and account settings

## Content Viewing Experiences

### 1. Browsing and Discovering Content (Browse Tab)

**User Interaction Flow:**

**Initial View:**
- When users open the Browse tab, they see a search bar at the top with the prompt "Search bills, cases, orders..."
- Below the search bar, there are four filter tabs: "All", "Bills", "Cases", and "Orders"
- The main area shows a scrollable grid of content cards

**Filtering Content:**
- Users can tap any of the filter tabs to see only that type of content:
  - **All**: Shows all content types mixed together
  - **Bills**: Shows only legislative bills (marked with "BILL" badge)
  - **Cases**: Shows only court cases (marked with "CASE" badge)
  - **Orders**: Shows only executive orders and government content (marked with "ORDER" badge)

**Searching:**
- Users can type in the search bar to find specific content
- Results update in real-time as they type
- Search works across all content types

**Content Cards:**
Each card shows:
- A visual indicator of the content type (BILL, CASE, ORDER, or NEWS)
- A title (truncated if too long)
- A brief description (2-line preview)
- A "Read More →" link
- Optionally, a thumbnail image on the right side

**Viewing Article Details:**
- Tapping any content card opens the full article in a detailed view

### 2. Consuming Content in Feed (Feed Tab)

**User Interaction Flow:**

**Initial View:**
- The Feed tab opens to a full-screen vertical feed
- Each item takes up most of the screen height
- Users see one article/video at a time

**Navigating the Feed:**
- Users swipe vertically to move between items
- The feed loads more content automatically as users scroll (infinite scroll)
- Each item shows:
  - Content type badge (BILL, CASE, ORDER, or NEWS) in the top-left
  - Article title and preview
  - A "Read Full Article" button at the bottom

**Viewing Full Articles:**
- Tapping "Read Full Article" opens the detailed article view
- Users can return to the feed by going back

### 3. Reading Detailed Articles (Article Detail Screen)

**User Interaction Flow:**

**Opening an Article:**
Articles open from either:
- Tapping a card in the Browse tab
- Tapping "Read Full Article" in the Feed tab

**Article Layout:**
The article screen has:
- A close button (X) in the top-left to return to the previous screen
- A content type badge in the top-right (BILL, CASE, ORDER, or NEWS)
- Two tab buttons below the header: "Article" and "Original"

**Two Viewing Modes:**

**1. Article Tab (Default):**
- Shows an AI-generated summary of the content
- Includes a disclaimer banner at the top: "This article was generated with AI and may contain errors."
- Presents the content in a clean, readable format

**2. Original Tab:**
- Shows the original source content
- Includes source attribution (e.g., "Source: Supreme Court")
- Has a "View on Original Site" button that opens the original webpage in a browser

**User Actions:**
- Users can switch between the "Article" and "Original" tabs to see different versions
- In the Original tab, they can tap "View on Original Site" to see the content on its original website
- Tapping the close button (X) returns them to where they came from

## Content Types and Identification

The app organizes content into four main types:

1. **BILLS** - Legislative bills and proposed laws
   - Identified with "BILL" badge
   - Typically government legislation

2. **CASES** - Court cases and legal proceedings
   - Identified with "CASE" badge
   - Includes judicial decisions and legal rulings

3. **ORDERS** - Executive orders and government content
   - Identified with "ORDER" badge
   - Includes presidential/executive actions and government documents

4. **NEWS** - General news articles
   - Identified with "NEWS" badge
   - Broader news coverage not fitting the other categories

## User Journey Examples

### Example 1: Following Legislative Activity
1. User opens the Browse tab
2. Taps the "Bills" filter to see only legislative content
3. Scrolls through bills of interest
4. Taps a bill about climate policy to read details
5. In the article view, switches to "Original" tab to see the official bill text
6. Taps "View on Original Site" to see the bill on the government website

### Example 2: Casual News Consumption
1. User opens the Feed tab
2. Swipes vertically to browse through different articles
3. Sees an interesting court case, taps "Read Full Article"
4. Reads the AI-generated summary in the "Article" tab
5. Returns to the feed to continue browsing

### Example 3: Researching a Topic
1. User opens the Browse tab
2. Types "immigration" in the search bar
3. Sees results across all content types (bills, cases, orders, news)
4. Uses the filter tabs to narrow down to specific types
5. Opens multiple articles to compare information
6. Uses both "Article" (AI summary) and "Original" (source) tabs for different perspectives

## Key User Interactions Summary

- **Tapping filter tabs** to view specific content types
- **Typing in search** to find specific content
- **Tapping cards** to open detailed articles
- **Swiping vertically** in the Feed to browse
- **Tapping "Read Full Article"** to open from Feed
- **Switching between "Article" and "Original" tabs** to see different content versions
- **Tapping "View on Original Site"** to see original source material
- **Tapping the close button (X)** to return to previous screens

## App Organization

The app presents government and legal content in an accessible format, with AI-generated summaries for quick understanding and original sources for detailed research. Users can choose between efficient browsing (Browse tab) or immersive consumption (Feed tab), with consistent access to both simplified summaries and original documents.
